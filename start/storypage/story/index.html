Start Page

chapter one

The journey

Spring 2009,

Tehran, Iran

We Were Hopeful...

The election was few days away and the country was in a state of excitement. It acted as a promisisng tool for a better future in people's minds, especially the younger generation. We were extremely excited to vote for the reformist candidate who promised change and a bright future especially for the young. The street were filled with positivity and hope. Hope for change...

I found my sister a few hours later. We were both safe but horrified and in disbelief about the events we witnessed that day...

The result brought millions of people on the streets, they were demanding answers about their votes. Peaceful demonstrations quickly turned violent and many got killed on the streets and in prisons.

We all decided that it was better for my sister and me to leave and continue our education in United States...

On the plane I kept thinking about all of the events that happened, about my family and friends, and about starting a new life abroad...

chapter two

The new place

We finally arrived at the airport in United States after almost 24 hours of flight, and the process of immigration at the point of entry took another 4 to 5 hours to complete.

However, we were soon met and greeted by our family friends, whom we had never met before. Their warm acknowledgements of our arrival made us feel happy and welcomed to our new “home”.

Our initial plan was to stay with them for a month or so until we could find our own place.

As we drove away from the airport, I began to realize the differences between my new environment and my hometown.

I began to notice evry little detail; everything was so different; highways were huge, and people seemed to obey the laws and regulation much more. All of it was new to me!

The city looked so beautiful; the lights, modern buildings and skyscrapers, the greenery. Everything looked very exotic and exciting, and this feeling of excitement lasted almost every day; there were many new things to explore!

Our friends accompanied us everywhere so we barely had to speak english. We would spend many hours at the malls, movie theaters, parks; overall, life seemed so calm and exciting!

Another highlight of our first month of stay was a trip to Disneyland, which was magical! Seeing all the castles and characters that I grew up watching was beyond exciting and I couldn't get enough of the land of fairytales...

Seeing everyone's excitement felt new and beautiful. Children and adults were wearing Mickey ears walking around the park. Everyone was smiling at each other; different characters were roaming the park, and magical fireworks and the music filled the air. It was my childhood dream coming true...

My life was so filled with new adventures that I barely thought about home in that first month... I kept thinking to myself that this new place was truley the land of magic and plenty...

Maybe I made the right decision!

chapter three

The challenge

After a month or so and with the help of our friends, we were able to find an apartment and move out. I vividly remember my feelings when we found ourselves in the empty new place.

I found myself surrounded by many unknown tasks. First, we had to buy necessary items for the apartment and set up required accounts with different companies for the monthly bills...

For days, I spend countless hours contemplating the online system for paying bills. Every time I tried to get some help on the phone, I found myself talking to a robot, and every single time, after much hassle, the robot managed to successfully explode my brain!

I was confused the first time I took the bus, which was on a very cold and windy day. The bus finally arrived after I waited for a long time, and I got on and sat on one of the seats.

Later on, I realized that I had to pay the moment I get on the bus, which was exactly the opposite of the process in my hometown! That was the reason the bus driver was so angry at me.

Every time that I wanted to say something, I had to translate my thoughts to English in my mind first, which was fairly a long process...

This usually resulted in an awkward silence followed by an awkward smile...

Over time, as a result of not being able to speak efficiently, I developed many different types of awkward and weird faces and smiles…!!!

As school started, I realized the huge difference between the school system in the U.S. and Iran. In the United States, everything was mostly done online, which caused so much stress and confusion for me.

After a few months my mom went back to my country, which was extremely hard for us. I started missing my family and friends more than before.

So, I would call my friends instead, which wasn’t easy because of the time difference…

I remember every time I spoke to them, all the feelings of disappointment, sadness and anger arose inside me, and I could tell that they were so worried about me…

As a result of all the hardship that I was going through, I became very depressed. I kept blaming myself for everything that was wrong. The city, the lights and amazing skyscrapers weren’t beautiful anymore… I was exhausted from the everyday battles….

And all I wanted to do was to sleep, and sleep and sleep…
